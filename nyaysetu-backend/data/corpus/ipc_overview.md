# Indian Penal Code — Common Sections (Plain-Language Summary)

This document summarises commonly cited provisions of the Indian Penal Code, 1860 (IPC) in
plain language. It is intended as study material; always verify against the bare act and
consult a qualified lawyer before acting.

## IPC Section 302 — Punishment for murder
Whoever commits murder shall be punished with death, or imprisonment for life, and shall
also be liable to fine. Murder is defined in Section 300 and is distinguished from culpable
homicide not amounting to murder (Section 304) by the presence of intention or specific
knowledge that the act is likely to cause death.

Practical note: A First Information Report (FIR) under Section 302 can be filed at the
police station having jurisdiction over the place where the offence occurred. Section 302
is a cognizable, non-bailable offence triable by the Court of Sessions.

## IPC Section 304A — Causing death by negligence
Whoever causes the death of any person by any rash or negligent act not amounting to
culpable homicide shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term
which may extend to two years, or with fine, or with both. This section commonly applies
to fatal road accidents and medical negligence cases.

## IPC Section 354 — Assault or criminal force on a woman with intent to outrage her modesty
Whoever assaults or uses criminal force to any woman, intending to outrage or knowing it
to be likely that he will thereby outrage her modesty, shall be punished with imprisonment
for a term which shall not be less than one year but which may extend to five years, and
shall also be liable to fine. The offence is cognizable and non-bailable.

## IPC Section 375 / 376 — Rape and punishment for rape
Section 375 defines rape; Section 376 prescribes punishment of rigorous imprisonment for a
term not less than ten years, which may extend to life. Survivors can file a Zero FIR at
any police station, regardless of where the offence occurred. Free legal aid is available
through District Legal Services Authorities.

## IPC Section 420 — Cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property
Whoever cheats and thereby dishonestly induces the person deceived to deliver any property
to any person, or to make, alter or destroy the whole or any part of a valuable security,
shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to
seven years, and shall also be liable to fine. This section is most commonly invoked in
fraud, online scam, and breach-of-trust cases. To file a complaint, lodge an FIR at the
local police station and preserve all evidence (messages, transaction receipts, emails).

## IPC Section 498A — Husband or relative subjecting woman to cruelty
Whoever, being the husband or the relative of the husband of a woman, subjects such woman
to cruelty shall be punished with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years
and shall also be liable to fine. Cruelty includes harassment for dowry. Complaints can be
filed at any police station or before a Magistrate, and survivors are also entitled to
remedies under the Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005.

## IPC Section 506 — Punishment for criminal intimidation
Criminal intimidation is punishable with imprisonment of up to two years, or with fine, or
with both. If the threat is to cause death, grievous hurt, or destruction of property by
fire, the term may extend to seven years. Document all threats (screenshots, recordings,
witnesses) before approaching the police.
